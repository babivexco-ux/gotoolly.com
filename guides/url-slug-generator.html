Professional Slug Creation Tips

üîó Tip: Use hyphens, not underscores - Search engines treat hyphens as word separators (how-to-guide) but treats underscores as connectors (how_to_guide). Hyphens are better for SEO.

üìè Tip: Keep slugs concise - Aim for 3-5 words (50-60 characters). Longer slugs get truncated in search results and are harder to read.

üî§ Tip: Always use lowercase - URLs are case-sensitive. Using lowercase prevents duplicate content issues and ensures consistency.

üéØ Tip: Include primary keywords - Place your most important keywords near the beginning of the slug for maximum SEO impact.

üìÖ Tip: Consider dates for time-sensitive content - For news or time-relevant articles, include the year (2026/seo-trends) to show freshness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use hyphens or underscores in URL slugs?

Always use hyphens (-). Search engines treat hyphens as word separators (making "how-to-guide" two words) but treat underscores as connectors (making "how_to_guide" one word). Hyphens are the web standard for readable URLs.

How long should URL slugs be?

Keep slugs between 3-5 words and under 60 characters. Longer slugs get truncated in search results, social media, and browser address bars. Focus on key keywords rather than complete sentences.

Should I include dates in URL slugs?

Include dates (year/month) for time-sensitive content like news, trends, or annual reports. For evergreen content, avoid dates to prevent the URL from appearing outdated. Example: /2026/seo-trends (good for news), /seo-best-practices (better for guides).

Can I change URL slugs after publishing?

You can change slugs, but always set up 301 redirects from old URLs to new ones. Changing URLs without redirects breaks links, loses SEO value, and creates 404 errors. Our tool helps you create optimal slugs from the start to avoid changes.

Should I remove stop words (the, a, an, and)?

Yes, remove common stop words to keep slugs concise. However, if removing a stop word changes the meaning or makes the slug confusing, keep it. "The-guide-to-seo" ‚Üí "guide-to-seo" (better), but "to-be-or-not-to-be" should keep the words.
