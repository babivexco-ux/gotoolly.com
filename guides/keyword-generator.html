Understanding Different Keyword Types

üéØ

Exact Match Keywords

Characteristics: Short, specific, high competition

  • Single words or short phrases
  • High search volume
  • Very competitive
  • Example: "SEO tools"

Best for: Brand awareness, competitive analysis

üé£

Long-tail Keywords

Characteristics: Longer phrases, lower competition, higher intent

  • 3+ word phrases
  • Specific user intent
  • Higher conversion rates
  • Example: "best SEO tools for beginners"

Best for: Conversions, specific queries, niche targeting

Question Keywords

Characteristics: Natural language queries, informational intent

  • Start with who, what, when, where, why, how
  • Informational search intent
  • Great for FAQ content
  • Example: "how to do keyword research"

Best for: Blog posts, guides, educational content

Frequently Asked Questions

How many keywords should I target per page?

Focus on 1-2 primary keywords and 3-5 secondary keywords per page. The primary keyword should be in your title, URL, and first paragraph. Secondary keywords should naturally appear throughout the content.

Should I target high or low competition keywords?

Start with low-competition, long-tail keywords to build traction. As your authority grows, gradually target more competitive keywords. A mix of both is ideal for sustainable growth.

How often should I do keyword research?

Regularly! Review and update your keyword strategy quarterly. Search trends change, new keywords emerge, and your business evolves. Continuous research ensures you stay relevant.

Can I use the same keyword on multiple pages?

Avoid targeting the exact same primary keyword on multiple pages (keyword cannibalization). Instead, create unique content for each keyword variation or focus on different search intents.

How do I know if a keyword is worth targeting?

Consider: relevance to your audience, search volume, competition level, commercial intent, and your ability to create better content than what's currently ranking.
